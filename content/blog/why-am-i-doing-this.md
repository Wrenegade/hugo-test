## The Reasons
So, why did I create seattlewren.com?  Let me list them out:

1. **I want to:**  I've been playing around with blog sites for over 10 years.  I've tried coding them up myself -- Wordpress, Django, Ruby -- all *worked* but failed.  I didn't like any of them and spent way too much time perfecting the look rather than writing content.  So, I never published any of them.  Now, with AI, anything is possible.
2. **I have empathy for governments:**  I spent 7 years in the social sector working with state governments for Early Childhood Care.  I've seen government (in)action and have empathy for them.  They need help from people like you -- and you probably don't know that.  I hope to educate through my social posts.
3. **Sharpens my AI:**  Building this site has sharpened my AI skills to where I can see the future with it.  AI is like my guitar -- the more I play it the better I get.  And I want to share what I've learned.  I hope you can see it from my point of view and pick up something along the way.
4. **Online Resume:**  I'm currently on a voluntary work pause and will be for most, if not all, of 2026.  So when someone asks "What did you do with your time?" -- here you go.
5. **For my kids:**  I have a better chance of them reading this site than having a conversation directly.  These are the things I want them to know -- today, and when I'm no longer around.

## My Motivation
There are a few.  Let's start with the selfish reason first.

**AI is an instrument**

AI is an instrument.  Like a guitar, piano, or set of drums.  It requires practice.  It has theory, fundamentals, and scales.  There are right chords and wrong chords.  It has rhythm and riff.  But, like your guitar, it won't play itself.  And if it does, the music will suck because it will be just some derivative of what's been done.  Not my taste.

That's how I see it.  An instrument, to be played -- by humans.

Think about when the synthesizer came out.  "That's not music!"  Well, some form of it exists in just about every song you hear today.  I recently learned that in Every Breath You Take, by The Police, the drums I've been air-drumming to for decades -- all drum machine.  Stewart Copeland programmed them in yet is still considered one of the greatest drummers to have ever lived.  How's that work?

Because it was still a human behind the machine.  AI is no different.  It just enables a human to create that much more.  Period.

I can play a few instruments -- guitar, piano, some bass.  Some better than others but I'm no Eddie Van Halen.  Never will be.  That's fine, I don't want to be.  I'm happy just practicing and being able to play the songs that I like.

With AI, I want to get good enough where I can play the *problems* that I like.

**The rabbit holes**
*"Shit be callin' me, man...it be callin' me.  I just gotta go to it."   Pooky, New Jack City.*

For me, it's crack.  I don't know if that's a good thing or bad but I'm a junkie for problem solving.  Habitual junkie.  I spent the last decade of my career in management and leadership, delegating the interesting work to my team -- that was the job and I did it well.  But you miss the deep end.  You miss scrambling through a problem never quite knowing where you'll come out the other side.

Now, with AI and a work pause, I start my day with coffee and a rabbit hole.  I'm not even picky about the problem, just as long as it gets me going.  If it does, I'll see you in a few days because I'm deep in those holes.  It's an addiction more than a want or need.  That's just the way I'm wired.

I like making analogies for complex problems.  It's the primary way I learn.  How does the complexity relate to my everyday?  Once I piece it all together in a story that I can visualize in a real world setting, I've got it.  A lot of this is just me learning about something and putting it into a familiar context.  It's never perfect and there are more devils in the details, but it's valuable for me and, hopefully, for you.

Whether my analogies are valid -- that's up to you to decide and criticize.  They make sense to me and I can put them to rest in my head.

But only after I've written them down.

## What To Take Away
Use it or lose it.  Roast me or "Prost" me.  I'll be curious either way.  If any of this is useful to you, let me know.  If there's something you'd like me to dive deep on, mention that too.  I'm not picky about the problems just as long as they capture my interest.  I'm happy to go down a rabbit hole and write about it to start a deeper conversation.

And yes, there will always be an AI plug.  I'm not getting paid by any of them.  At least, not yet.  But I do think it's the new world order -- no, I *know* it is -- and one that will transform the globe.  The barrier to entry just got blown up and the land is shifting, making way for a new surf spot.  Whether people come with me or not, I'm paddling out.

I'm not missing these waves.
