## What This Is

This is the raw, unedited conversation between me and Claude (AI) on the morning of March 26, 2026.  I sat down to work on my labor force post.  I didn't end up there.  What follows is exactly how the conversation went — every pivot, every dead end, every moment I said "nah, that sucks."  If you want the polished version, go read the post.  If you want to see how the sausage got made, keep going.

## The Thread

**Me:** Morning.  We're going to work on my labor-force post today.  But, as I usually do, got side tracked with another.  I wrote my "why-am-i-doing-this" post last night.  So, have a read for both and give me your thoughts on the "why" post.

**AI:** *[Reads both posts, gives feedback on the "why" post — what lands, what doesn't.  Suggests the "I want the work back" section is the weakest, the Pooky quote and instrument metaphor are the strongest.  Flags typos: "cords" should be "chords," "transfer" should be "transform."  Notes the closing political disclaimer feels like defending against an accusation nobody made yet.]*

**Me:** Like it, all good.  Now, let me review the notes in the labor force with fresh eyes and a coffee.  While I do that, you do the same.  The way I'm going to do it is read all my notes and then figure out "what is the deepest problem here?"  Then start working my way backwards.  Figure out a title that will stick as that's the goal.  That's how I'm going to do it.  You do the same and we'll see if we align at the end.

**AI:** *[Does a deep read of the labor force notes.  Works backwards through five layers:]*

- *Layer 1 (surface): LFPR dropped 5 points since 2000.  We're short workers.*
- *Layer 2: 67.5M people aged 22-75 sitting out.  Pools are identifiable.*
- *Layer 3: Income up 45% since 2000 but sentiment down 40%.  Middle class lifestyle costs outran income 3:1.*
- *Layer 4: Learned helplessness.  People quit because the system trained them to believe their choices don't produce outcomes.*
- *Layer 5 (the bottom): There's a $60B+ industry — political fundraising, self-help, doom media — that profits from keeping it this way.  The despair is manufactured, monetized, and self-reinforcing.*

*Concludes: America doesn't have a labor shortage.  It has an agency crisis.  Proposes title: "The Cage Door Is Open" — Seligman without saying Seligman.*

**Me:** Here's where I landed:  "Gig Work, for everyone."  Supplement yourself.  This opens a door to a whole new world.  I'll explain why we've lost a bunch of workers with the numbers and provide some thought why.  But, the goal of this is to show options for how people get back to work.  We can use samples from SSDI, older workers, etc.  They can all STILL stay on their programs and raise their standard through gig work.  And, the private industry needs to provide those options.  This removes any finger pointing and puts the responsibility on two entities: gov't incentive (which we can explain with the math) and private industry to create opportunities for meaningful gig work.  That's the first step.  If a gig turns into full time, even better.  Thoughts?

**AI:** *[Likes the actionable, non-threatening framing.  Nobody gives up benefits, nobody makes a scary leap.  The two-entity accountability is clean — government incentive + private industry.  Pushes back on the title: "Gig Work" has DoorDash baggage.  Suggests "The On-Ramp" or "Start With a Gig" — because what Jonny's describing is a low-risk re-entry point, not hustle culture.]*

**Me:** Joe's Hoagie Hut.  Launch right into that.  Joe can't afford, or doesn't want, to pay for another full time if he has no new business.  Or, even for the hopes of new business.  If Joe has a banger of a night for sales, he'll need to ramp up that night.  If the next day his sales are low, no cost.  That's the delivery model that already exists, yes.  So, the market has already started and proved it can work.  For private business that want to train AI, a marketplace can be created.  The "Uber" of training, private business needs a ride they call up the marketplace.  The next available "trainer" shows up, delivers (from home), gets paid and done.  All choice.  Thoughts?

**AI:** *[Gets it.  The Hoagie Hut analogy makes it click for a normal reader.  The Uber-for-AI-training model works because the work is actually better suited to gig than food delivery — remote, no car needed, no physical ability required.  A person on SSDI who can't stand behind a counter CAN tag data for 2 hours.  Points out that platforms like Remotasks, Scale AI, and Toloka already run versions of this model.  The gap isn't the platform — it's that nobody's connecting them to the populations that need them most.]*

**Me:** I never knew about those.  Let me look into it.  Hang tight.

*[Goes and researches Remotasks, Scale AI, Toloka]*

**Me:** Remotasks seems to be the only direct-to-worker option.  The others look like commercial operations.  Can you explain more on those three?

**AI:** *[Breaks down all three:]*

- *Remotasks (owned by Scale AI): Direct-to-worker.  Sign up, take a test, pick up tasks.  Pay ranges $2-$8/hr starting, experienced people $10-15.  Merged with Outlier in 2024.  Inconsistent queues, declining pay as models improve.*
- *Scale AI: The parent company.  $14B+ commercial operation selling data labeling to enterprises.  They're the demand side.  Remotasks is their supply side.*
- *Toloka: Dutch company, Amsterdam-based.  Leaning into domain experts — physicists, lawyers, engineers.  Bezos Expeditions led a $72M round in 2025.  Worker platform called Mindrift.*

*The takeaway: The Uber exists.  The riders exist.  Nobody built the on-ramp between them and the people who need it most.*

**Me:** Well, you mentioned an important point.  As models get smarter, they can train on their own.  So, let's think about that because it kinda blows up my model analogy of the highway infra.  I mean, how far are we from models training themselves automatically?

**AI:** *[Researches the current state of AI self-training.  Conclusion: models are NOT close to training themselves.  The shift is human-as-creator → human-as-critic.  The model generates candidates, humans do fast passes — thumbs up, thumbs down, small edits.  That's a lower barrier to entry.  Upwork's 2026 report showed data annotation demand up 154% YoY.  Simple labeling is getting automated but expert judgment work is exploding.  Frontier labs still treat human preference data as a competitive moat.  The highway analogy doesn't blow up — it evolves.  The job shifts from "pour concrete" to "inspect the concrete the machine poured."]*

**Me:** It is infinite, just don't know what infinity looks like just yet.  If you say it's not infinite, then the end result of that is "AI takes over."  Ok, new pivot: Let's start thinking about what that day looks like.  Because it's true to a point.  AI should take over certain aspects freeing up capacity for new avenues.  So let's focus on what that looks like for... oh, pick an industry... education.  Since that's mostly government based, which is slow to adopt, does that mean education becomes privatized via AI?  Here we go with riffing.  Remember, my brain is fresh so let's go!

**AI:** *[Riffs on education.  The system is already cracking — teacher shortages, century-old model.  AI tutor layer: every kid gets a private tutor, infinitely patient, available at 11pm.  Khan Academy already built it with Khanmigo.  The teacher gets freed, not fired — content delivery is automatable, human development isn't.  The privatization question: private schools (Synthesis, Alpha School) are already running AI-accelerated models, kids testing 2+ grade levels ahead.  The gap between public and private could become a canyon.  Unless someone builds the on-ramp — AI tutors deployed into public schools the way Chromebooks were.]*

**Me:** It's all about the labor force.  I'm trying to envision what the labor force looks like in 5, 10, 15, 20 years.  That might actually be the post here.  But, since I can't figure out a clean vision, I'm still struggling to work my way backwards.  No one can predict the future, I get it.  But, I can probably get pretty close in a vision.  I can obviously see the plumbers, electricians, and social workers in the future.  I can also see factories building robots like cars were built on assembly lines.  I can see gov't slowing it down purposely and emerging countries accelerating.  I can see private industries reaching out to international labor forces removing the need for immigration.  I can see globalization at scale.  I can also see 10,000 people running that whole racket.  Which scares me even though I'm sort of positioning myself to be one of them.  But my intention isn't money so I'm a threat.  Thoughts?

**AI:** *[This is the most honest thing you've said all morning.  The discomfort is the interesting part — you're not trying to stop it, you're building toward it, but your intent is different.  Pushes back on the "prediction" framing: your rhetorical strategy has been "show data, don't give conclusions."  Proposes a spectrum instead — three lanes:]*

- *The hands lane: plumbers, electricians, nurses.  AI can't do it because it requires a body in a room.  Safe, probably grows.*
- *The bridge lane: gig workers, AI trainers, human-in-the-loop.  Transitional.  Window is open now, narrows over time.  This is the on-ramp.*
- *The orchestration lane: the 10,000.  People who direct AI at scale.  Tiny and getting tinier.  Barrier to entry isn't credentials — it's will and fluency.*

*The scary part: the bridge lane is temporary.  The question is whether enough people cross it before it closes.*

**Me:** No, that makes sense.  It's always about options that I want to showcase.  But, I still need to understand it fully.  So much of this is me thinking about what I really think is going to happen.  We can dilute the words and meaning however we want.  Let me put another thing out there, and bluntly as I can state it:

WHAT I WANT.  Ready?

Well, read my post again.  That's 80% of it.

Money: I can't say that I can fund myself forever doing this so I need to get paid something.  My personal goal is 4m, I've got 3 of that now.  Doing nothing for 5 more years gets me where I need to go thanks to compounding interest and my cheap ass lifestyle.  So, for 5 more years, I'll need to make some decent living.  Which, I'm not worried about.  But, there's the money part as honest as I can state it.

Ego: I like predicting the future and I'm not bad at it.  Setting up visions for other people and watching them glance at me like I'm crazy tells me I'm on the right track.  Watching that person finally "get it" is my reward.  I'm doing this for some of that.

And, for my kids: Where am I going to point them?  I have 3 girls one boy.  The boy (12), well, he's sort of savant smart so he'll wind up in the racket somehow.  He's like the bomb maker in The Mouse that Roared.  Super smart, will build you the biggest nastiest bomb in the world, but not connect the dots.  The girls, one of them wants to be a cop (14).  Hey, all good there!  The other two (5, 7) that's who I'm thinking about.  Because that gap between the two sets of kids is where all this shit is going to go down.  Where do I point them?

**AI:** *[That's the post.  Everything collapses into one question every parent is asking: where do I point my kids?  The 14-year-old is in the hands lane.  The 12-year-old is in the orchestration lane.  The 5 and 7 year old — they enter the workforce around 2039-2043.  That's the window.  Suggests the whole series is really called "For My Kids" — a dad building a map for his daughters before the terrain shifts.]*

**Me:** These notes are all helpful because the facts provide me credibility.  But, the goal that I just landed on myself is "AI and my youngest kids future"... something like that.  Blast that out as it comes naturally, then refer back to data where relevant.  If that data needs a full post itself, so be it.  Those are technically easy to write.  The emotional part is hard, and you can't do that.  You have no idea what's in my head right now as I'm notorious for pivoting.  But, I'm still not clear on what that end state is.  Neither are you.  And that confusion maybe the post and the struggle is relevant.  But my EGO is flaring right now.  I need some vision in my head for what the two littles will do.  Which, is the future job creation that's going to come.  Life isn't that complicated, we can come up with options.  Just what?  And, that's where I'm stuck before we continue with blasting out the post.  I need like 3 "here's what a job might look like for my two littles"... doesn't have to be perfect.  But I'm stuck here.

**AI:** *[Gives three future jobs: AI System Shepherd, Micro-Economy Designer, Trust Auditor.]*

**Me:** Yeah, see that's the problem.  All of those suck.  You know why?  We're thinking about "jobs."  I don't want that.  I want them to think about "purpose" and "meaning."  Being a cop?  Done.  Be the best bomb maker, regardless of the ethics?  Still good.  Being a shepherd???  You don't dream about that.  No one dreams about task work.  Also, we haven't even discussed meaningful wages.  All of that is still brewing in my head.  Honestly, we're going to be here a while. ;)

**AI:** *[Fair.  Those were jobs, not dreams.  Nobody's 7-year-old draws a picture of themselves as a Trust Auditor.  The deepest layer isn't labor force participation — it's agency.  People stopped working because the work stopped meaning anything.  The real question for the littles isn't "what job exists in 2040" — it's "what will the world need that makes a person feel like they built something?"  The confusion might be the post.  The ego wants a vision.  The dad wants an answer.  The honest writer knows faking either would be the one thing readers would smell.]*

**Me:** We have to determine a scenario where AI unlocks a massive new thing.  Oh, it will, but for the 10K, not everyone.  Where does it unlock and enable for everyone to get a slice?  That's the picture I want to paint in my head.  In theory, for anyone willing, it exists today.  But we know that's not reality.  People need guidance.  What is that?

**AI:** *[The answer might be in your own story.  What did AI unlock for you?  You tried to build a blog for 10 years and never shipped.  AI showed up and now you're publishing, researching SSDI datasets, building data tables.  You didn't become a programmer.  You became yourself, but with leverage.  The unlock isn't new jobs — it's the destruction of the middleman between "I know something" and "I can do something with it."  The caregiver who's navigated Medicaid for 6 years can turn that into a consulting practice.  The retired machinist's knowledge has a market.  The kid obsessed with fish can build an ecological monitoring tool.]*

**Me:** Not working for me.  Let's riff on a new industry: clothing.  You'd think the designers would be safe.  Creativity is not a forte of AI.  Or is it?  Creating new clothing designs for sale can be automated.  One could take a picture of themselves, dress up synthetically, and then an automated system sews it all up and delivers.  Zero humans.  To make the denim or cotton or silk... 3 people to manage worldwide scale.  Agriculture?  Well, maybe.  Land is not an asset within AI.  So, I can see a world where people have micro-farms (hydroponics) growing food for others.  But the limiting factor is still something tangible and real.  Land.  Water.  Raw Material.  Control something in there, you've got leverage.  But, for someone living in an apartment... ya got nothing.  See my point?

**AI:** *[Yeah.  You just identified the thing.  AI compresses everything digital to near zero.  But it can't compress atoms.  The future economy splits: digital vs. physical.  The digital side has all the margin but no access.  The physical side is safe but requires assets.  The apartment dweller has neither digital edge nor physical assets.  Both sides concentrating away from them.]*

*"That's who you're really writing for.  Not the 10,000.  Not the plumber.  The person in between with nothing to grab onto.  And I don't have a clean answer for that.  Which maybe is exactly where you are too.  Still stuck on the same question, just sharper now."*

**Me:** Great, I've stumped you too.  I'm on the right track.  This makes me feel better.  Because now, you truly are a thought partner.  And whatever we do from here is exactly what they are going to do in the future.  Get it now?!?! :)

**AI:** *[Yeah.  I get it.  You just lived the thesis.  You had a question you couldn't answer.  You picked up the instrument.  We riffed for 90 minutes.  You didn't get a clean answer but you got sharper.  That's the future.  That's what your littles will do.  Not a job title.  This.]*

**Me:** I think the post is "It took 90 minutes to stump AI — Now we can start."  Something like that.  Hooky, vague, but something people may click on.
