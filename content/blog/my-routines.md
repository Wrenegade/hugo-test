Routines are how I control my life.  Trust me when I say this, I'm not one of those junkies that gets up, drinks a gallon of water, does yoga for an hour then eats an avocado toast with handfuls of supplements.  I'm not that guy.  And that's not this post.

But I am pretty disciplined about a few things.  At least, for most of the year.  However, because I can rely on routines I've built over the years, it allows me to pivot off of them for a period of time without regret.  I know I can go back to the routine because it's durable and built for the long haul.  That's what I want to share with you.  Hope some of this helps.

## My Routine Stack
The one thing that may truly annoy you is that I am a morning person.  Always have been as far as I can remember.  I get bored sleeping.  I know I need it but, candidly, I wish I didn't.  I like doing stuff -- and that requires me to be awake.

With that said, here is the normal day-to-day.  This goes whether I'm working or on a [work pause](https://jonnywren.com/blog/work-pause/).
### Routine #1:  Hit the floor then to-do list
1. Get out of bed, no later than 6am:  Here's the annoying thing, I'm usually out of bed by 4:30am.
2. Make that pot of coffee
3. Simple stretch:  Not full on yoga shit, just a couple of stretches that force me to the carpet.  *Hitting the floor is the important part*.  Because it actually gets some blood moving, not just pulling a leg back holding onto the table. 
4. Check the weather:  This determines a LOT for how I conduct my day
5. Review To-Do list:  My favorite part of the morning

Between 5am and when the kids get up, that's my most "alert" time of the day.  My brain got rest and is usually at it's most creative point of the day.  I take advantage of that.

New ideas, new projects, travel plans, calendar planning -- all happens during this timeframe.  For us "agile-planners" this is where I review, edit, and update my backlog of work.  This includes anything from writing new blog posts, repotting garden starts, and making dinner for the family.  Also, pending the weather, determines where I will work.  Again, currently on a work-pause, I can just work from my home office.  

But, that home office is also my music studio...all my gear is in there.  It's very easy to get sidetracked, especially if something on my to-do list for the day is "learn that new song".  So, i hit up libraries as they are free, quiet, and creates a productive setting.  I love libraries.

To summarize:  I get up, make coffee, quick stretch (on the floor), check weather, the hit the to-do list.  Once I feel like I've got a solid to-do list, then I consider that the **start** of my day.  All of this happens between 5am and 7am.

## Routine #2:  Re-assess my to-do list, adjust the backlog
Yep, after all that, I look at my list and say "there's no way all that's going to happen"

I had just spent an hour setting it up perfectly.  Then, I immediately cherry pick the one or two things that I actually will do that day.  Why?  Because I know the day will not be perfect.  Not even close.

I set up my to-do list with such vigor in the hopes that I'll have the most productive day ever.  The reality is, only about 20% of that plan ever gets done.  Sometimes, none of it gets done.  I'll roll it all over to the next.
