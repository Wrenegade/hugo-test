## What You're Looking At

The U.S. unemployment rate.  It's calculated by dividing the number of people actively looking for work by the total labor force.  Currently 4.4%, which looks healthy.

Important note: The number only counts people who are actively searching.  Since 2000, roughly 7 million working-age Americans stopped searching entirely.  They don't show up in this chart's line — but they show up in the red dashed line at 8.5%.

The shaded regions highlight the same economic eras as the debt-to-GDP chart: post-war booms, defense buildups, financial crises, and the pandemic.  Notice how the headline rate always recovers.
