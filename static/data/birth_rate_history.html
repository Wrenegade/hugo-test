U.S. Birth Rate

1940–2024  |  Births per 1,000 population. Each generation made fewer kids than the one before.

Sources: CDC / NCHS Natality Data · World Bank via FRED (SPDYNCBRTINUSA)

25.0 per 1,000 1957 — Peak of the Baby Boom. Post-war optimism, GI Bill, suburbs, single-income households that could afford a house and four kids.
16.7 per 1,000 1976 — The trough. Birth control pill, women entering the workforce, Roe v. Wade, stagflation. Boomers were having fewer kids than their parents.
11.0 per 1,000 2022 — All-time low. Housing costs, student debt, delayed marriage, economic uncertainty. Gen Z isn't even starting.
