U.S. Unemployment Rate

1948–2026  |  The headline number that everyone quotes. It looks fine. That's the problem.

Sources: BLS via FRED (UNRATE)

4.0% April 2000 — "Full employment." Sentiment high, confidence high, income high. Everything lined up. The benchmark.
14.7% April 2020 — COVID peak. Highest since the Great Depression. The number recovered. The people didn't all come back.
4.4% February 2026 — Looks almost identical to 2000. But sentiment dropped 40%, 7 million left the labor force, and nobody's counting them.
